March 15, 2025

University Ranked Among Top 10 in Kenya

Softcore University has been ranked among the top 10 universities in Kenya according to the latest rankings released by the Education Commission of Kenya.

The prestigious ranking places Softcore University at position 8 nationally, marking a significant improvement from last year's position at 12. The ranking considers several factors including academic reputation, research output, student-to-faculty ratio, and graduate employability.

Vice Chancellor Dr. Jane Mwangi expressed her delight at the achievement: "This recognition reflects our commitment to academic excellence and innovation. Our faculty and students have worked tirelessly to elevate our standards, and this ranking validates those efforts."

The university particularly excelled in the engineering and technology categories, with its Electrical Engineering program being ranked 3rd nationally.

April 10, 2025

New Engineering Complex Inaugurated

The university has inaugurated a state-of-the-art engineering complex equipped with modern technology to enhance teaching and research capabilities.

The new 8-story Engineering Innovation Center was officially opened by the Cabinet Secretary for Education, featuring cutting-edge laboratories, smart classrooms, and collaborative workspaces. The KSh 2.3 billion facility is the largest infrastructure project in the university's history.

Key features include:

  • Advanced robotics lab with industrial-grade equipment
  • Renewable energy research center with solar and wind testing facilities
  • 3D printing and rapid prototyping workshop
  • 200-seat auditorium with teleconferencing capabilities

Dean of Engineering Prof. James Okoth noted, "This complex positions us as a regional leader in engineering education. Our students will now train with equipment matching what they'll find in industry, giving them a competitive edge."

May 13, 2025

15th Annual Research Conference Announced

The university will host its 15th Annual Research Conference on May 13-20, 2025, with the call for papers now open to academics worldwide.

This year's theme is "Innovation for Sustainable Development" with keynote speakers including Nobel laureate Dr. Wangari Muthoni and Google AI lead Dr. Kwame Johnson. The conference will feature:

  • Over 50 parallel sessions across disciplines
  • Poster presentations by graduate students
  • Industry roundtables connecting researchers with private sector
  • Startup pitch competition with KSh 5 million in seed funding

Conference chair Dr. Amina Said emphasized the international scope: "We've received abstract submissions from 28 countries already. This is becoming a premier platform for African research to connect globally."

Early bird registration is available until March 30th, with special rates for students and delegates from developing countries.
