About Electrical & Electronics Engineering

Electrical and Electronics Engineering (EEE) is a field that deals with the study and application of electricity, electronics, and electromagnetism. Our program provides students with a solid foundation in both electrical systems and electronic circuits, preparing them for diverse careers in industries ranging from power generation to consumer electronics.

The EEE program at Softcore University is accredited by the Engineering Board of Kenya and recognized for its strong emphasis on practical skills development through laboratory work, industrial attachments, and hands-on projects.

Program Objectives

By the end of the program, graduates will be able to:

  • Design and analyze electrical and electronic systems
  • Apply engineering principles to solve complex technical problems
  • Develop innovative solutions for power systems and electronic devices
  • Work effectively in multidisciplinary engineering teams
  • Understand the ethical and professional responsibilities of engineers

Specialization Tracks

In the final two years, students can choose to focus on one of the following specialization tracks:

Power Systems Engineering

Focuses on generation, transmission, and distribution of electrical power. Courses include power system analysis, high voltage engineering, and smart grid technologies.

Electronics & Embedded Systems

Concentrates on electronic circuit design, microcontrollers, and embedded systems development. Includes courses in analog IC design, FPGA programming, and IoT systems.

Telecommunications Engineering

Emphasizes communication systems, wireless technologies, and networking. Covers digital communications, microwave engineering, and fiber optics.

Laboratories & Facilities

Our Electrical and Electronics Engineering students have access to state-of-the-art facilities:

  • Power Systems Lab: Equipped with transformers, generators, and protection systems
  • Electronics Lab: Featuring oscilloscopes, signal generators, and prototyping stations
  • Digital Systems Lab: With FPGA development boards and microcontroller kits
  • High Voltage Lab: For studying insulation materials and high voltage phenomena
  • Renewable Energy Lab: With solar panels, wind turbines, and energy storage systems
